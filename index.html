The Problem We're Solving

Every year, millions of elderly people experience falls. For families, the worry is constant: What if something happens when I'm not there?

Traditional fall detection solutions like smartwatches and pendants have high false alarm rates and are often worn on wrists or necks, far from the body's center of mass, leading to unreliable detection.

OPORA is different. Worn on the chest, close to the center of gravity, our device uses advanced AI to distinguish real falls from everyday movements with exceptional accuracy.

Why OPORA Works Better

Chest-Mounted Design

Positioned near the body's center of mass, our sensors capture movement data that's far more representative of actual falls, dramatically reducing false alarms.

Transformer AI Model

We use a deep learning model based on the Transformer architecture, the same technology behind modern AI breakthroughs, to analyze sensor data with remarkable precision.

Multi-Sensor Fusion

Accelerometer, gyroscope, barometer, and ECG sensors work together, processed through a Kalman Filter, to understand exactly what's happening to the wearer.

Instant Notifications

When a fall is detected, the device alerts the wearer with sound and immediately sends push notifications to family members through our mobile app.

The Device

OPORA is built on the Raspberry Pi Zero 2W platform, making it accessible, hackable, and perfect for DIY enthusiasts. The custom PCB module integrates all necessary sensors in a compact, wearable form factor.

How It Works

1

Continuous Monitoring

The device continuously collects data from the accelerometer, gyroscope, barometer, and ECG sensor while worn on the chest.

2

Signal Processing

Raw sensor data is filtered using a Kalman Filter to remove noise and artifacts, ensuring clean signals for analysis.

3

AI Analysis

Every 8 seconds of data is analyzed by our Transformer-based deep learning model trained to recognize fall patterns.

4

Instant Alert

If a fall is detected, the device sounds an alarm and sends immediate notifications to designated family members.
